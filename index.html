01
Brand Strategy
The foundation: why UniQ exists, what it stands for, and the principles guiding every creative decision.
Mission
Why We Exist
To create an inclusive, safe, and empowering community for LGBTQIA+ students and allies at Te Herenga Waka, Victoria University of Wellington, where every person belongs.
Vision
Where We Are Going
A university campus where takatapui and queer students thrive, connect without barriers, and leave feeling more themselves than when they arrived, with UniQ as the heart of that experience.
Values
What We Stand For
Transparency, Reliability, Consistency, Responsibility. These are the four core principles UniQ brings to everything it does, from events to advocacy to community care.
Tagline
Unapologetically You
Short, inclusive, action-oriented. Flexes to context: "Be Unapologetically You" for campaigns, "Unapologetically UniQ" for internal culture.
Brand Story
Made With Aroha
UniQ is a community group providing queer students a place to belong. Every design decision, every word, every event reflects genuine care for the people in the room.
Positioning
Where UniQ Sits
Warm and activist. Neither corporate nor chaotic. The brand should feel like it was made by and for queer students, not produced by a marketing department.
Core Values
🌈
Inclusion
Every LGBTQIA+ identity is valid and celebrated. We make space intentionally, especially for those most marginalised.
Non-negotiable
🔥
Authenticity
We show up as we are. Imperfect, evolving, joyful. Our brand reflects real people, not a polished corporate persona.
Core
✊
Advocacy
We do not just celebrate; we fight. UniQ actively advocates for queer rights within university structures and beyond.
Active
💛
Community
Connection over content. Everything we do is designed to bring people together, reduce isolation, and foster lasting friendships.
Foundation
🌱
Growth
We learn, evolve, and listen. Our language, practices, and positions improve as we grow alongside our community.
Ongoing
🎉
Joy
Being queer is something to celebrate. We balance serious advocacy with genuine fun, warmth, and playfulness.

Colour Usage Rules
Do
  • Use the full rainbow gradient as a feature element: headers, backgrounds, borders
  • Use individual colours for tinted chips, tags, and section accents
  • Pair any gradient stop with white text (check contrast first)
  • Use Lavender (#a381da) as the single most-used standalone brand colour
  • Keep backgrounds light (white, off-white) to let the gradient stand out
Do Not
  • Place light colours (#efc26b, #90ce76) over white, as contrast fails accessibility
  • Use all 6 gradient colours simultaneously as separate competing elements
  • Introduce non-brand colours without explicit sign-off from the committee
  • Use the gradient on every element, as it loses impact
  • Place #cd9bc6 over white for body text (fails WCAG AA)

Personality
Warm and Welcoming
Feels like a message from a friend, not a university department. Inclusive language, no jargon, no talking down.
Personality
Playful and Real
Comfortable with emoji, humour, and informal language when the context calls for it. Authentic over polished.
Personality
Bold and Unafraid
Speaks clearly about LGBTQIA+ rights. Takes positions. Uses correct terminology. Never vague when it matters.
Personality

Warmth and care are in the name, literally signed off with aroha in UniQ's own content. Gentleness is a feature.
Personality
Joyfully Queer
Queerness is something to celebrate. The voice should pulse with joy, because joy is a form of resistance.

ContextToneExample LanguageAvoid

Do
  • Use "they/them" as default singular pronoun in copy
  • Spell out LGBTQIA+ in full on first use per document
  • Use "queer" positively and proudly (it's been reclaimed)
  • Use inclusive greetings: "Hey everyone", "Kia ora koutou"
  • Reference Te Reo Maori naturally, including aroha, waka, koutou
  • Sign off community content "With aroha, UniQ" where appropriate
  • Use THW (not Vic) when shortening the university name
Do Not
  • Binary or exclusionary greetings ("guys", "ladies and gentlemen")
  • Conflating gender identity with sexual orientation
  • Acronyms without expansion on first use
  • Passive voice in advocacy statements (own the position)
  • Tone-policing or minimising community concerns
  • Outdated terminology: check current community standards regularly
  • Em dashes: use commas, periods, or parentheses instead

Motion and Animation
How UniQ moves: principles, timing, easing, and practical guidance for digital motion.
Principle 1
Joyful, not distracting
Animation should add delight, not noise. Every motion should have a purpose. When in doubt, animate less.
Principle 2
Springy and energetic
Use spring/overshoot easings for interactive elements. They feel alive. Avoid linear or purely ease-in transitions (too mechanical for UniQ's personality.
Principle 3
Respect accessibility
Always honour prefers-reduced-motion. Provide static fallbacks for all animated content. Never use auto-playing video without controls.

Imagery and Photography
Visual style guidance for photos, illustrations, and graphic elements used across UniQ materials.
Photography Style
Real, Candid, Joyful
Authentic photos of real UniQ members are always preferred over stock. Show genuine moments of connection, celebration, and community. Colour-grade warmly, with a slight boost in saturation to complement the brand palette.
People Photography
Representational and Inclusive
Feature diverse identities, expressions, and body types. No forced posing. Candid is better. Always get explicit consent for any photography used in brand materials.
Illustration Style
Flat, Rounded, Colourful
When using custom illustrations (event posters, social assets), use a flat vector style with rounded shapes that echoes the logo's organic geometry. Pull colours strictly from the brand palette.
Icons
Rounded and Consistent
Use a single consistent icon set (Lucide, Phosphor, or Heroicons with rounded stroke weight 1.5-2px). Never mix icon styles. Colour icons with brand palette fills or use outlined on white.
Graphic Elements
Fluid, Organic Shapes
Background blobs, organic dividers, and wave shapes (as seen in the business card design) reinforce UniQ's playful energy. These should use gradient fills from the brand palette.
Image Overlays
Subtle Gradient Washes
When placing photos behind text, use a semi-transparent gradient overlay (5-15% opacity) in brand colours to maintain visual cohesion. Never use solid black overlays alone.
Do
  • Use real event photos (they build trust and authenticity)
  • Warm colour grade: +15 saturation, +5 warmth in post
  • Show genuine emotion: laughter, hugging, pride
  • Feature Pride flags, rainbow accessories naturally in context
  • Get written consent from all identifiable subjects
Do Not
  • Use stock photos of generic "diverse" groups (feels fake)
  • Heavily filter photos to the point of looking staged
  • Crop or centre non-white subjects as "diversity props"
  • Use photos that accidentally out anyone
  • Use cold, blue-heavy colour grades (off-brand)

Accessibility Standards
UniQ is for everyone. Accessibility is not optional. These are the minimum standards for all brand materials.
Visual Accessibility
Colour and Contrast
  • Normal text: minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio (WCAG AA)
  • Large text (18px+ bold, 24px+ normal): minimum 3:1
  • Never convey meaning through colour alone; always add text or icons
  • Avoid placing light gradient colours over white for text
  • Test every design with a colour blindness simulator
Typography Accessibility
Readability
  • Minimum 16px for all body text on screens
  • Line height minimum 1.5 for body paragraphs
  • Maximum line length 75 characters for comfortable reading
  • Never use justified text alignment, as it causes spacing issues for dyslexic readers
Digital / Web
WCAG 2.1 AA
  • All images must have descriptive alt text
  • All interactive elements must be keyboard-navigable
  • Focus states must be clearly visible (never removed)
  • All video content must have captions
  • Respect prefers-reduced-motion in CSS
Content Accessibility
Inclusive Language
  • Use plain language (aim for Year 8 reading level) in community comms
  • Provide content warnings for potentially distressing topics
  • Offer text alternatives to visual-only information
  • Use structured headings in all digital documents (H1, H2, H3)

Who owns the brand, how it is approved, and what to do when you need to create something new.
Brand Stewardship
Who Is Responsible
👑
UniQ Committee: responsible for using assets correctly per this guide
🎨
Social Media Officer: maintains brand files, reviews visual assets
📣
Publications Officer: approves voice/tone of public-facing copy
Approval Process
Before Publishing
1
Create asset using brand templates in Canva
2
Self-check against this brand kit
3
Share with Social Media Officer for review (advocacy content: Publications Officer too)
4
Publish or hand to print supplier
Asset Storage
Where to Find Files
🎨
Canva Brand Kit: live templates, locked brand colours and fonts (Lexend Deca + Pikimisa)
📁
Google Drive / shared folder: exported PNGs, SVGs, PDFs ready to use
📧
Contact info@uniqthw.org.nz for access or missing files
Third-Party Usage
Partners and Sponsors
  • Sponsors may use the UniQ logo in co-branded materials with prior written approval
  • Always provide logo files directly; never send editable source files to external parties
  • Co-branded materials must be reviewed and approved by UniQ committee before publishing
  • Unauthorised use of UniQ branding should be reported to the committee immediately
